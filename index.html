Our lives are tracked thoroughly, deeply, and ways that are not always obvious. Through our daily behavior, we create documents of ourselves: where we go, what we do, what we wonder, what we long for, what we worry about.

These documents, intimate and expansive, provide a personal archive of a scale never before possible. But there is one catch – this archive is about us, but not for us. Instead, this information is fed through complex processing systems that are infinitely more efficient than any one of us. This data – mine and yours, and everybody else’s in aggregate – goes on to power the information economy.

I believe these documents may serve to benefit us, too. Your little human brain may not be able to parse your archive as efficiently as Google can, but it can do other things.

Imagine a journal of every time you’ve looked for answers to questions big or small. What would it look like?
